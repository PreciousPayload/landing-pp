About The client company:

“Using supermagnets for space applications gives us more control of the skies, keeps them clean, and revolutionizes space travel”

said Max Arashavsky, CEO and founder of Zenno Astronautic

Zenno Astronautics was born at the University of Auckland (New Zealand). A group of top engineering students asked themselves, what if space exploration wasn’t limited by fuel? They got to work developing an engine that could overcome this obstacle through the use of fuel-free, safe electromagnetic technology. The result? The Zenno’s magnetic engine, dubbed the SuperTorquer, can move in any direction, expanding the any craft in orbit.

Zenno’s innovation creates very high magnetic field densities and dipole moments onboard a spacecraft without consuming essential energy, and at almost zero cost. It can be used for complex maneuvers—like docking and rendezvous—and also for shielding payloads and crews during travel between planets or even while working on the Moon. All of these tasks are easily and efficiently done using Zenno’s knowledge and application of electromagnetic fields.

Case study: using Precious Payload to make feasibility study

Increasing the technology readiness level (TRL) of your technology is key to securing the funding for your company and converting those Letters of Intents in real revenue. Design of an orbital demonstration mission for the payload has to be closest to the future commercial use regime, provide ample of verifiable data, and made as fast as possible with minimum risks. In order to pass the critical design review, a mission design team has to work closely with external mission contractors starting from mission feasibility study.

Due to the lack of secure collaboration tools that allow sharing technical data externally, today space industry satellite engineers spend up to 12 months designing the mission before bringing it in front of the potential suppliers. In 3/4 of the cases, the design team has to redesign the entire mission from scratch based on the feedback they get from contractors. An average space startup loses 2.5 years and up to $1.5 millions to unnecessary rework and back-and-forth review calls with contractors.

Solution

Zenno engineers came up with 2 architectures of their technology demonstration mission design of a magnetorquer: a 3U CubeSat and a hosted payload. Using Precious Payload, they spent less than 2 hours together with our team to list requirements for both architectures and create a KYC profile for the company. The software then matched their requirements with hosted payload and launch opportunities, and Zenno team specified their procurement preferences, such as budget, risk tolerance, and timeframe.

The Request for Proposal was sent to 80% of the global launch providers (more than 25 companies). Verified sales engineers and business development managers from around the world were able to access the data room, verify the client, and leave their feedback in form of questions and comments specific to individual data points in mission design. After a round of requirements negotiations happening on the platform between Zenno and suppliers engineers without any Zoom calls or meetings, Zenno gets 7 negotiated and verified proposals and shortlists 3 suppliers for pre-contract in less than 4 weeks.

features

Mission Data Room

One of the key features of Precious Payload is Mission Data Rooms — a secure place to store mission requirements. It allows to list mission level, spacecraft level, and service level requirements using best NASA/ESA practices and standards.

During the mission design phase, Mission Data Rooms allows an engineering team to work on multiple concurrent mission designs. For example, you can design a same mission as a free flying CubeSat or a Hosted Payload using mission templates in less than 30 minutes.

Views and Requests

After finalizing the requirements, you create a Request to be submitted to an external collaborator or a supplier. Precious Payload has templates to run Requests for Information or Proposals (RFI/RFP) to launch operators, hosted payload providers, insurance brokers, componentt manufacturers, and ground segment providers. In order to keep trade secrets close to your heart and comply to export control regulations you need to share what is absolutelly necessary for your supllier to work with. This is why we developed a feature called Views.

When sharing a Request to a supplier, Precious Payload software creates an encrypted collaboration space called View and suggests which fields from the Mission Data Room should be included based on the type of service requested. A know-your-client (KYC) profile of your company is attached to the View alongside the automatic Non-Disclosure-Agreement that a supplier signs upon accessing the View. A View creator has full visiblity and audit trail into which user got access to the information in a View, and every login is authorized via a one-time code send to a verified mobile number for maximum security.
